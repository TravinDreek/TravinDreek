What's changing and why

Visitors to this site probably know me from some proprietary video games such as Minecraft, my skills at modding these games, and my interests in computer programming. People like me are sometimes called "tech nerds".

I used to be a tech nerd who unwisely focused on what technologies could do rather than how they did it, so I had fun playing all kinds of video games and surfing around every social media site. However, I don't do them any more - I gave up Discord, Twitter, and many other things, and I don't play Minecraft or other games that are proprietary software any longer. Why?

With my continuous diving in the field of computing and programming, I came to the conclusion that most technologies that we make use of today pays no respect to the essential freedom and privacy that we deserve to have, and that in order to make our first step in control of our digital life, we need Free Software, which is the software that you have the freedom to use, modify and share.

Now I have put all my passion into promoting free software and advocating digital rights. And I believe that with the freedom that free software comes with, we can fight against censorship, surveillance, and the unjust power of big tech.

I'm not going to cover all the philosophy behind it in this memo, but you should probably read gnu.org so that you understand why I made such a decision without compromise.
